## Research Methodology

### How to Conduct Research

#### 1. Reddit Research
```
Subreddits: r/programming, r/webdev, r/devops, [domain-specific]
Search queries:
- "frustrated with [competitor]"
- "alternative to [competitor]"
- "how to solve [problem]"
- "[feature] recommendations"
```

#### 2. Product Hunt Research
- Check top products in category
- Read comments for feature requests and complaints
- Track launches of competitors

#### 3. Stack Overflow Research
- Search for questions related to our domain
- Identify common pain points from question patterns
- See what workarounds people use

#### 4. G2/Capterra/Trustpilot Reviews
- Read 1-star and 2-star reviews (what breaks the experience?)
- Read 5-star reviews (what delights users?)
- Identify patterns across multiple reviews

#### 5. User Interviews
- Prepare script with open-ended questions
- Record and transcribe (with permission)
- Look for emotional language ("frustrating", "love", "hate")
- Ask "why" 5 times to get to root cause
